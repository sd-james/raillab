My research curiosity was sparked by playing around with algorithms for RoboCup 3D soccer simulation , watching agents figure out how to work together in constraint environment got me hooked on the bigger question: how do groups of agents actually cooperate and coordinate under pressure?

My research sits at that intersection, focused on building teams of agents that can collaborate and make smart decisions in fast-moving, dynamic environments , the kind where things change faster than any single agent can handle alone.

Outside of research, I run the Wits Mathematical Sciences Support Data Science Interest Group, where I bring that same energy for problem-solving to the broader student community.
